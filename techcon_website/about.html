History

2010 – Humble Beginnings:
TechCon was founded in 2010 as a small gathering of passionate tech enthusiasts in Nairobi. It began as a grassroots effort to bring together like-minded individuals who believed in the power of technology to drive change. Early events were characterized by intimate workshops, spirited discussions, and a focus on learning and collaboration.

In the years that followed, TechCon steadily grew in size and scope. The conference began to attract a wider audience, including startups, students, and professionals eager to share ideas and network. This period cemented TechCon’s role as a catalyst for knowledge sharing and community building within the regional tech ecosystem.

2015 – Expanded Horizons:
By 2015, TechCon had transformed into a prominent platform for innovation, featuring expert panels, interactive demos, and keynote addresses from international leaders in technology. The conference’s expanded programming reflected a broader mission: to make emerging technologies accessible to all and to nurture cross-disciplinary innovation. These years saw TechCon increasingly highlight the ingenuity and creativity of African technologists and entrepreneurs. Local startups showcased their solutions to real-world challenges, from fintech and healthtech to agri-tech and renewable energy. The event became a place to celebrate African innovation and connect it with global partners.

2020 – Adapting to a New Era:
The global pandemic in 2020 forced TechCon to pivot to a virtual and hybrid format. Despite these challenges, the event thrived, reaching participants across the continent and beyond. Virtual keynotes, online workshops, and hybrid networking sessions underscored TechCon’s resilience and adaptability. In recent years, TechCon has solidified its reputation as a movement, not just a conference. It has become a global platform to advocate for responsible, inclusive, and sustainable technology development. Today, TechCon continues to connect global and local leaders, foster a collaborative community, and inspire the next generation of tech talent.

Mission

At TechCon, our mission is to catalyze innovation and empower the future of technology in Africa and beyond. Anchored in Nairobi’s vibrant tech ecosystem, we bridge global and local players, showcase African ingenuity, and inspire the next generation through mentorship and knowledge exchange. We champion sustainable and inclusive tech growth, fostering a collaborative community where ideas thrive and partnerships flourish. TechCon is not just a conference—it’s a movement shaping the technological landscape of tomorrow.

Past Speakers

TechCon has been honored to host visionary leaders and pioneers from across the globe, whose work has profoundly impacted the trajectory of technology. Here are a few examples of notable global speakers who have graced our stage in previous years:

Dr. Fei-Fei Li

Dr. Fei-Fei Li is a renowned computer scientist and AI pioneer, widely recognized for her work in computer vision and cognitive neuroscience. As the co-director of the Stanford Human-Centered AI Institute, she has been a leading voice in advocating for ethical and inclusive AI development. Dr. Li’s groundbreaking research has advanced how machines understand visual information, laying the foundation for many modern AI applications.

Ms Gwyne Shotwell

Ms. Gwynne Shotwell is the President and COO of SpaceX, where she has been instrumental in driving the company’s vision of making space travel more accessible and sustainable. With her expertise in engineering and business, Ms. Shotwell has overseen the launch of numerous missions, including collaborations with NASA and global satellite deployments. Her leadership has played a crucial role in transforming the aerospace industry and inspiring future generations of innovators.
